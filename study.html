2. Hund's Rule

Electrons prefer to occupy separate orbitals with parallel spins before pairing. This maximizes the total spin and follows the principle of maximum multiplicity.

3. Pauli Exclusion Principle

No two electrons in an atom can have identical quantum numbers. This means each orbital can hold at most two electrons with opposite spins.

‚ö° Ion Formation: Cations vs Anions

Cations (Positive Ions)

Atoms lose electrons to form positively charged ions. The order of electron loss follows specific rules:

üéØ Electron Loss Priority

  1. Highest n value first - Outer shell electrons go first
  2. Highest l value second - p electrons before s electrons
  3. Exceptions apply - Consider stability of resulting configuration

Anions (Negative Ions)

Atoms gain electrons to form negatively charged ions, typically to achieve octet stability.

üéØ Electron Gain Strategy

  • Fill available orbitals following Aufbau principle
  • Achieve octet when possible
  • Consider energy levels for stability

üîí The Octet Rule

Atoms tend to gain, lose, or share electrons to achieve 8 valence electrons. This configuration provides maximum stability.
